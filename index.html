Weird Gloop is the company behind the RuneScape and Old School RuneScape wikis. We founded it in 2017 after increasing concern and disagreement with the previous wiki hosts, and since then we've worked hard to improve the wiki experience for everyone.

We're players of RuneScape, just like those who use our sites, and our wiki editors work hard day in and day out to preserve the high quality of the content we have. We've made a ton of improvements over the last year since we launched the wikis, and we'd like to share them with you.

New wikis

In early 2019, we welcomed the Portuguese-Brazilian RuneScape Wiki and the RuneScape Classic Wiki to our network. The Portuguese wiki has been consistently maintained by a group of dedicated editors, and we thought it would be a great idea to bring them on board. While RuneScape Classic is no longer available, we think it's only right that we preserve the history of RuneScape - and do so under one roof - which is why they're also part of our family.

Milestones

The past year has been a blast for the wikis. We've made improvements across the board, from creating a dark theme from scratch, to working with Jagex and third parties on various integrations.

  • 2 October 2018

    New site launches

    On a clear afternoon in Cambridge, UK, we launched the brand new RuneScape and Old School RuneScape Wikis. While there were a few minor teething problems to begin with, players were quick to move over to the new sites.

  • 31 October 2018

    Dark mode is released

    Our number one request after launch - as we predicted it might be - was releasing a dark mode for the wikis. Later in the month we were happy to make this a reality, and now dark mode is used by [x] people across both wikis.

  • 13 December 2018

    OSBuddy adds wiki integration

    For the first of our third party integrations, we worked with OSBuddy to allow Old School RuneScape players to lookup various items, objects, and more from the comfort of the client.

  • 14 February 2019

    RuneLite adds wiki integration

    After 2019 kicked off, we were keen to touch base with RuneLite and create a plugin which features functionality including look ups and even searching the wiki using an in-game interface.

  • 17 March 2019

    One Small Wiki Favour launches

    We're constantly looking out for dedicated new editors which can help us improve our wikis, so across the RS and OSRS Wikis we launched One Small Wiki Favour, a programme which encourages users to make contributions to various projects and receive bonds in-game for their work.

  • 20 May 2019

    Jagex adds in-game wiki integration

    We were incredibly excited for integration with the official RuneScape client, which launched in May this year. Players can use /wiki [search term] in the in-game chat interface to open the RuneScape Wiki.

  • 5 October 2019

    RuneScape Wiki hosts panel at RuneFest

    In a RuneFest first, we hosted a panel about all things wiki featuring various administrators. We had an amazing time! Check out a recording of the livestream here!

Technical

With new power comes new responsibility, and our sysadmins have worked hard on ensuring that you can view our sites without a hitch, while adding powerful new enhancements.

Difficulties during launch week

Despite being incredibly excited about our launch, we did have a few problems to work with. Almost immediately after the new wikis went live, we experienced ridiculously high load - as a result of in-game announcements and such - which grinded our servers to a halt. The load evened out after a while, but we wanted to make sure that we would not suffer problems down the line.

We actually launched the wiki with a minimal number of servers. A lot of requests were hitting our origin, so we quickly spun up more servers to cope with demand, and we invested in working with providers like Cloudflare.

One of our sysadmins has written a blog post about our move.

Article caching for anonymous users

Over the last few months, we've invested time and money into improving our performance even more. One of the services we now utilise heavily is Cloudflare Workers.

We were already using Cloudflare to distribute our content and act as a load balancer, but we found that we can use it in a much more powerful way to store entire pages inside of their key-value store.

Quite simply, if you're logged out and you view the RuneScape or Old School RuneScape Wiki now, the chances are you will be served a copy of the page from this store. Your request will never hit our origin servers, and the page will load very quick. Rather than your browser trying to retrieve a wiki page from our servers in Europe, you will be served cached versions from your closest Cloudflare datacentre, of which there are 194.

A few seconds after someone edits a wiki page, those changes propagate worldwide. You're always benefitting from a super fast browsing experience on our wikis.
