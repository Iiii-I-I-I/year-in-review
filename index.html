Weird Gloop is the company behind the RuneScape and Old School RuneScape wikis. We formed in 2017 after major concerns with the previous wiki host, and since then we've worked nonstop to provide the best possible wiki experience for the players.

2019 has been a great year for RuneScape, and a great year for the wikis. We've forged new collaborations to make us the best possible resource for RuneScape and Old School RuneScape. We take pride in the quality of the wikis, and we'd like to share this year's successes with you.

New wikis

In early 2019, we welcomed the Portuguese-Brazilian RuneScape Wiki and the RuneScape Classic Wiki to our network. The Portuguese wiki is maintained by a group of dedicated editors, and we thought it would be a great idea to bring them on board. While RuneScape Classic is no longer available, it is still part of the legacy of the game. Our goal is to preserve the history of RuneScape under one roof, and Classic is part of our family.

Milestones

The past year has been a massive success for the both the RuneScape and Old School RuneScape wikis. Let's take a closer look at some of the major achievements we've had during 2019.

  • 5 January 2019

    Update coverage

    The wikis started off the year strong with complete coverage of early January updates. RuneScape featured a complete Mining and Smithing rework, and Old School RuneScape developed the new Kebos Lowlands region. Just hours after release, our editors had already prepared articles, taken images, and written guides.

  • 14 February 2019

    RuneLite integration

    The Old School RuneScape client RuneLite released a wiki plugin to help users quickly pull up articles on items, NPCs, and quests.

  • 2 March 2019

    RuneLite drop rates

    RuneLite provided the Old School RuneScape Wiki with 50 million kills of drop log data. This resource has enabled the wiki to calculate accurate drop tables for hundreds of monsters that would never otherwise have been possible.

  • 17 March 2019

    One Small Wiki Favour

    Across the RS and OSRS wikis we launched One Small Wiki Favour, a programme to reward new editors with bonds in-game for their contributions to the wiki. This has helped dozens of new editors get started, and helped complete numerous wiki projects.

  • 21 April 2019

    Discord collaboration

    We have affiliated with other Discord communities to improve our coverage on specialized topics. The PvM Encyclopedia community have helped us write high-level PvM guides on RSW, and the RuneScape Speedruns community have provided high quality speedrunning guides for OSRS. The wikis are always looking out for dedicated teams to collaborate with on any subject.

  • 20 May 2019

    Jagex integration

    The official RuneScape client was updated to add integration with the wiki. Players can now type /wiki in the chatbox to easily search for wiki articles from directly in-game.

  • 16 September 2019

    Perks calculator

    The RuneScape Wiki released a 100% accurate Perks calculator! Perks had long been poorly understood, so figuring out the exact mechanics was a big accomplishment. This would not have been possible without the help of Shauny.

  • 5 October 2019

    RuneFest wiki panel

    In a RuneFest first, the wikis hosted a panel to discuss all things wiki. The panel featured administrators from both wikis. RuneFest was a huge opportunity to talk with Jagex Moderators, which provided invaluable collaboration on a number of wiki projects. Check out a recording of the livestream here!

  • 14 November 2019

    Twisted League coverage

    Old School RuneScape launched Twisted League, a seasonal competition in Great Kourend and the Kebos Lowlands. The wiki prepared a collection of guides and annotated the entire wiki with Twisted League icons. On the day of release, thousands of players utilized the wiki to explore Twisted League from the very beginning.

  • 13 December 2019

    RuneScape events interface

    The RuneScape Wiki launched a convenient events interface directly at the top of the site. The interface shows useful timers for dailies, as well as the current Voice of Seren and featured activities. Check it out today by clicking the calendar icon!

Technical

With new power comes new responsibility, and our sysadmins have worked hard on ensuring that you can view our sites without a hitch, while adding powerful new enhancements.

Difficulties during launch week

Despite being incredibly excited about our launch, we did have a few problems to work with. Almost immediately after the new wikis went live, we experienced ridiculously high load - as a result of in-game announcements and such - which grinded our servers to a halt. The load evened out after a while, but we wanted to make sure that we would not suffer problems down the line.

We actually launched the wiki with a minimal number of servers. A lot of requests were hitting our origin, so we quickly spun up more servers to cope with demand, and we invested in working with providers like Cloudflare.

One of our sysadmins has written a blog post about our move.

Article caching for anonymous users

Over the last few months, we've invested time and money into improving our performance even more. One of the services we now utilise heavily is Cloudflare Workers.

We were already using Cloudflare to distribute our content and act as a load balancer, but we found that we can use it in a much more powerful way to store entire pages inside of their key-value store.

Quite simply, if you're logged out and you view the RuneScape or Old School RuneScape Wiki now, the chances are you will be served a copy of the page from this store. Your request will never hit our origin servers, and the page will load very quick. Rather than your browser trying to retrieve a wiki page from our servers in Europe, you will be served cached versions from your closest Cloudflare datacentre, of which there are 194.

A few seconds after someone edits a wiki page, those changes propagate worldwide. You're always benefitting from a super fast browsing experience on our wikis.
